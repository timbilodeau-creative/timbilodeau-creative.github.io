About Me

Building worlds since childhood, from dirt to desktop.

I’m a game designer based in Kelowna, B.C., with a background in art and tech. I graduated from Toronto Film School’s Video Game Design & Animation program in 2021.

Age: 3

Commodore 64 and BASIC

Discovered computing through my grandfather’s Commodore 64—learning BASIC commands, booting games from 5¼-inch floppy disks, and exploring every function of the console and joystick for hours on end.

Age: 5-10

Windows 3.1, 95, 98 and XP

The golden era of curiosity and computers. I was always first to the family PC, diving into Flash and Java games, experimenting with programs, and smuggling DOS games to school on 3.5-inch floppy disks and early USB drives.

Age 11-15

Windows XP & 7. Software and Web Dev

Excelled in PowerPoint, Word, and Excel during grades 5–6, then advanced to web development in grades 8–9, learning HTML, CSS, and basic JavaScript—skills that would soon guide me toward game design

Age 15-20

Windows 10, Linux & Others

As my skills grew, I moved from web development into 3D art and game creation. What began with basic Blender lessons in school quickly turned into late nights experimenting on my own, uncovering Unity Engine, and learning how full game development pipelines work. That spark pushed me to take courses and join local game dev communities.

Age 20-Present

Formal Education, Networking and Specializing

Graduated from Toronto Film School with a diploma in Video Game Animation & Design. Immersed myself in local game development communities and joined Global Game Jam events, turning years of self-taught curiosity into hands-on collaboration and creative growth.

Future

Collaboration, Creativity and CT & Fang

Growing within a passionate studio environment while developing my original IP, CT & Fang. Focused on collaboration, innovation, and creating experiences that inspire curiosity, connection, and wonder.
